THERE IS A DIRE NEED FOR INDIVIDUALS TO LEARN HOW TO PRESERVE THEIR PERSONAL HISTORY AND RECORDED MEMORY.

In 2016, the DC Public Library introduced their Memory Lab as a scalable model to address these needs. A year later, the library received an IMLS National Leadership Grant for the Memory Lab Network, a four-year project aimed at building preservation capacity across the U.S. through the creation of other Memory Labs. The Space

A free do-it-yourself digitization and transfer lab with state-of-the-art and rare equipment.

The Online Resource

Step-by-step instructions on using the lab, as well as further reading on personal archiving and preservation. For DC Public Library's online resources, we also have resources on how to build your own Memory Lab.

The Community Events

A series of ongoing events related to all of these topics. For example, workshops on preserving your family photographs, classes on preserving your social media accounts, Home Movie Day screenings, and other events celebrating local communities and their recorded memory.
